# Introduction: Planning a Power Apps project

If you're new to Power Apps, creating an app from a blank screen might feel overwhelming.

You might have an idea, but you might not have thought through all the details and information you need to create an app. You might be a business user or IT pro who has never designed or built an app. Or you might be a full stack developer who wants to learn how working with Power Apps is different from traditional app development.

In these articles, regardless of your background or how much you know about app development, you learn the steps to convert your ideas into a fully working solution using Power Apps.

## Suggested approach

The basics of making an app are as follows:

1. [**Plan**](planning-phase.md): Identify the who, what, when, and why.

1. [**Design**](designing-phase.md): Model the data (decide how the data you need to create is to be organized, accessed, and stored) and sketch out the app screens.

1. [**Make**](making-phase.md): Create the app.

1. [**Test**](testing-phase.md): Invite users to try the app.

1. [**Deploy and refine**](discoverability.md): Get your app to more users, gather feedback, and decide what to change or add.

This set of articles explains each of these steps. A detailed example of an expense reporting app clarifies the concepts.

## Audience

If you're a *businessperson*, these articles help you plan and execute what might be your first app project:

- The [Planning phase](planning-phase.md) helps whether you're planning to build an app yourself or create requirements to share with another app maker. You're familiar with many of the planning steps, but some might be new to you.

- The [Designing phase \> Conceptual design](app-tasks.md) helps you turn your business process into the screens and features of an app.

- If you're planning to create your own app, the [Designing phase \> Architectural design](where-is-data.md), [Making](making-phase.md), [Testing](testing-phase.md), and [Deploying and refining phase](discoverability.md) sections are easy to follow for new app makers.

If you're a *dedicated app maker* who isn't an expert in the business process:

- Follow the [Planning phase](planning-phase.md) steps while interviewing your future app users, to help you understand the business process.

- The [Designing phase](designing-phase.md), [Making phase](making-phase.md), [Testing phase](testing-phase.md), and [Deploying and refining](discoverability.md) sections all include Power Apps-specific information to help make your project successful.

If you're a *full stack* developer who is new to Power Apps:

- Read the next article, [Differences between Power Apps and traditional app development approaches](app-development-approaches.md).

- Then, if you're already familiar with app project planning, follow the [Designing phase \> Architectural design](where-is-data.md) steps. This section contains material specific to Power Platform.
